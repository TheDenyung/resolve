## ⚙️ What is resolve?

resolve is an Android app that handles customer support chats automatically. It uses artificial intelligence to reply to messages on your behalf. Everything runs on your device, so there is no need for an internet connection after installation. The app does not send your data to any server or cloud service.

This means your chats stay private and secure. You can trust resolve to assist with common customer questions quickly and without delay.

---

## 🧩 How does resolve work?

resolve uses AI models built into the app to read and respond to support chats. It connects with your Android accessibility service to control chat apps. The app learns to understand messages and suggest replies based on common customer support topics.

Because all processing happens inside your phone, resolve works offline. It does not require you to sign up or connect to any external system.

---

## 📱 System Requirements

To use resolve, your device needs to meet these conditions:

- Android version 9.0 (Pie) or higher  
- At least 2 GB of RAM  
- 50 MB of free storage space  
- Basic chat or messaging app installed for customer support  
- Accessibility service enabled (resolve will guide you through the setup)

### 3. Enable App Installation from Unknown Sources

Before you can install the APK, you need to allow your device to install apps from sources other than the Google Play Store.

- Open your Android **Settings** app  
- Go to **Security** or **Privacy** settings  
- Find and enable **Install unknown apps** for your file browser or web browser app where you downloaded the APK  

Your device may show a warning about installing apps from unknown sources. This is standard when installing apps outside official stores.

### 4. Install resolve

Once the APK download finishes, open the file. Your device will ask to confirm installation.

Tap **Install** to begin. The process should take a few seconds. When done, tap **Open** to run resolve for the first time.

### 5. Configure Accessibility Service

resolve requires permission to observe and control other apps to manage chats.

- resolve will prompt you to open **Accessibility Settings** automatically.  
- Find **resolve** in the list of installed services.  
- Enable resolve by toggling the switch.  
- Confirm any warning messages.

This step allows resolve to work with your messaging apps correctly.

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting

If resolve does not respond to messages as expected:

- Check that the accessibility service remains enabled.  
- Make sure the chat app you use is supported and running.  
- Restart your phone to refresh all services.  
- Confirm that resolve has permission to run in the background and is not restricted by battery saver.  
- Update to the latest resolve version from the release page.
